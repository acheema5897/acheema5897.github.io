How Humans Differ from AI when it comes to learning through reinforcement


Introduction


My final project covers the research question of how humans differ from artificial intelligence (AI) in reinforcement learning. I will explain how deep reinforcement learning models used in AI are designed after human behaviour psychology and neuroscience.


Artificial Intelligence (AI)


Artificial intelligence (AI) to put simply is the idea of simulating human intelligence with technology. Programmers simulate human behaviour in particular learning through a concept under AI called Machine Learning.


Machine learning


Machine Learning is a broad concept that attempts to simulate human behaviour through various algorithms. For example, there exist genetic algorithms designed after biological evolution to solve tasks. However, the particular machine learning algorithm I will be discussing in this project is reinforcement learning which is modelled after the human reward mechanism (Littman, 2023).


Reinforcement Learning (RL)



Reinforcement Learning is a type of Machine Learning algorithm that simulates human intelligence through decision-making influenced by a reward mechanism as designed after behavioural operant conditioning and neuroscience.


To put it simply, there is an agent (robot, human) that interacts with an environment (digital, real) using policies that help form future decisions based on previous actions and their consequences.


A fairly common decision-making model used in reinforcement learning AI is Markov’s Decision Processing (MDP) model (Subramanian et al, 2022).








Classical and Operant Conditioning


Both Classical and Operant Conditioning have their origins in behaviour and neuroscience in biological creatures as associative involuntary and voluntary responses to stimuli and consequences respectively.


Classical Conditioning is the concept behind associating an involuntary action like drooling to a stimulus like food as demonstrated in the case of the famous Pavlov's dog experiment.


Operant Conditioning is the concept behind associating a voluntary action with consequences and rewards which is backed by neuroscience through the chemical dopamine produced in biological reward mechanisms (Cherry, 2023).


Reinforcement Learning makes use of a neuroscience understanding of Operant Conditioning to compute a decision (policy) in response to consequences (rewards). How exactly does Reinforcement Learning Modeled AI make decisions is through a process called Markov Decision Process (MDP).




The Turing Test by Alan Turing


The Turing Test produced by British Scientist Alan Turing is a historically famous benchmark to test Artificial Intelligence as the concept behind AI is to simulate human behaviour.


Alan Turing’s Turing Test focuses on a particular aspect of human intelligence which was human conversations by testing if AI could deceive an actual human into thinking it is talking to another human (Turing, 1950).
