Introducing the Visualization

Zhenbang Feng’s interactive visualization of sorting algorithms illustrates how different sorting algorithms sort varying data sets, in particular, I will be covering the sorting algorithm “Selection Sort.”


The keyword “Iteration” carried a similar meaning throughout history which was the repetition of tasks and this follows into Sorting Algorithms as their job is to execute repetitive tasks in order to sort a data set.



Visualization Analysis


Time Complexity Confusion

While the visualization does a good job of explaining what a sorting algorithm is and how each algorithm provided works there are still terms like time complexity which are left ambiguous to those without knowledge in computer science.



Time complexities are formulated by analyzing the number of comparisons made during the execution of said algorithm. Zhenbang should dedicate a section to time complexities in order for viewers to have the context required to understand the visualization.


While you can slow down Zhenbang’s simulation there is nothing that indicates when a comparison was made which could help clarify to viewers how the time complexity was calculated.



Additionally, the time complexities Zhenbang provides are all average cases which in itself isn’t bad but I believe there could be more clarity by presenting what a worse or best-case time complexity would look like alongside its respective data set.


Poor Colour Scheme Choice

There are 3 different colour schemes for these data sets and by revisiting Zhenbang’s Visualizer site you may notice that 1 is randomly selected.


Out of the 3 colour schemes, only 1 option is colour-blind friendly as tested using the site colour-blindness.com.



Another improvement would be making the text contrast as the black text sometimes blends with darker tones of colours.


Concluding Analysis

To conclude, there could be more clarification on what time complexities are alongside a more colourblind-friendly colour scheme.


While it is known that sorting algorithms and iterations are repetitive tasks with goals I have learned that there are many different ways of going about repeating a task as shown in the comparisons of “Selection Sort.”
